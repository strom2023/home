Flash Tech Chronicles

FLASH TECH CHRONICLES

The primary objective of the " FlashTech Chronicles " program is to equip participating students with practical expertise in proficiently connecting electrical components, comprehending circuit dynamics, and understanding circuit functionality. By the culmination of this program, students will possess the skills necessary to deliver theoretical solutions to intricate problems in a manner accessible to individuals with varying levels of technical knowledge. This objective is designed to foster a comprehensive understanding of electrical circuits, encompassing practical skills, theoretical comprehension, and effective communication of solutions, all of which align with academic and professional development goals.

Timing : 10.30 to 12.30

Rules and Regulations:

  • Team Composition:
    • Teams must comprise 2 to 3 participants.
    • Registration and member confirmation should be done in advance.
  • Problem Selection:
    • Teams choose problem statements randomly from provided lot papers.
    • Problem statements remain sealed until teams open them.
  • Preparation Time:
    • Teams are allotted 10 minutes of dedicated preparation time after receiving the problem statement.
    • No use of electronic devices or external resources during preparation time.
  • All participants are requested to bring their college ID card and Bonafide certificate from their respective colleges, and it is one among the particulars of the entry ticket for the symposium.
  • All the participants are advised to address themselves in the symposium with a neat formal dress code.
  • Students are asked to bring their ID cards and should follow proper dress code.
  • Only students taking an engineering course are allowed to take part in this event.
  • Oral Presentation:
    • Each team must deliver solutions in a clear and concise oral presentation.
    • Presentation time must not exceed 5 minutes.
  • Visual Aids:
    • Teams are allowed to use visual aids (diagrams, charts) to support their presentations.
    • Visual aids should be relevant and clear in relation to the problem statement.
  • Judging Criteria:
    • Evaluation is based on creativity and feasibility of proposed solutions, effectiveness of 5-minute oral presentations, and ability to convey complex ideas understandably.
